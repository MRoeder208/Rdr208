Teaching

In New South Wales, the TAS (Technology and Applied Studies) teaching areas for Years 7-10 cover a broad spectrum of practical and theoretical subjects designed to equip students with essential skills and knowledge. These areas typically include introductory courses in technology, design and technology, and various aspects of applied studies, emphasizing hands-on learning and problem-solving.

In Years 7-10, the TAS (Technology and Applied Studies) subjects typically include:

  • Design and Technology - Focuses on the design process, material properties, and practical project work.
  • Digital Technologies - Covers coding, digital systems, and information technology skills.
  • Food Technology - Includes cooking techniques, nutrition, and food safety.
  • Textiles Technology - Emphasizes fabric properties, textile design, and garment construction.
  • Engineering Principles - Introduces basic engineering concepts and problem-solving techniques.
  • Graphics Technology - Involves creating and interpreting technical drawings and visual representations.

These subjects aim to provide a broad foundation in practical and technological skills.

For Years 11 and 12, the TAS curriculum expands to include specialized courses such as Industrial Timber and Metal, and Engineering and Construction. The Industrial Timber and Metal course focuses on advanced techniques in working with timber and metal, enabling students to develop practical skills in fabrication, machining, and design. Meanwhile, the Engineering and Construction courses delve into the principles of engineering and the construction industry, offering insights into structural design, building processes, and project management. Both of these senior courses prepare students for careers in technical fields and further education in specialized areas.
